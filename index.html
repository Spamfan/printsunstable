Take Home Sheet Guide

Click on each carrier to see an example filled out.


Give this to postpaid customers before they leave. On each sheet:

  • Write the customer's name, carrier name, account pin, and phone number.
  • In bill estimates, round up to the nearest "worst-case-for-customer" dollar amount.
  • Assure the customer they can generally leave the carrier when they want, the amount shown of 2 or 3 years only indicates how long it would take them or their carrier to pay off the financed device(s) at that rate.
  • The entire "important notices" section at the bottom can be entirely cut off for T-Mobile customers since it won't become relevant.

For ATT/VZW papers only:

  • Assume ALL bill credits WON'T go through until after bill 1.
  • Additionally showing the customer how high ATT/VZW will overestimate their bill is important, since most Walmart bill credits don't become visible to the carrier until about the time the second bill becomes pulled.
  • In the examples shown, the amounts expected to be reimbursed to bills 2 and 3 are represented by "x."
    --- Example, if ATT gives back the whole $35 act fee on bill 2, that one would be 58-x, in this case 58-35=23. However, they may send it to Bill 3 instead, so bill 2 would remain as 58 with bill 3 as 58-x=23. There can also be a mix where x is spread between multiple bills , such as Bill 2=33 and Bill 3 =48.
